Thanks to its competitive EcoCar2 team, Rose-Hulman has garnered its largest in-kind gift to date, a $27.8 M 3-D modeling software suite (pictured above) from Siemens PLM Software. The gift will enable undergraduate engineers to create next-generation designs on the most advanced industry tools. With 98% placement among the class of 2011 and a 20% increase in companies recruiting at this year's Fall Career Fair, Rose-Hulman students are poised for strong career opportunities. Read about the options that faced future-bound Rose-Hulman students at Fall Career Fair, 2011.

Rose-Hulman celebrated the primacy of innovation with a packed Hulbert arena for last weekend's Homecoming speaker, famed physicist and innovator Dr. Michio Kaku. After receiving the Excellence in Innovation award, Dr. Kaku helped launch the  new Innovation Center in a ribbon-cutting ceremony. Soon, the 16,000-square-foot space bustled with student-led projects, competition teams, and cultural displays. Images.

In an interview for Dallara Magazine, Rose-Hulman president Matt Branam cited collaboration, long-term thinking and "learning by doing" as the formula that drives quality innovation. Gian Paolo Dallara (above rt.), of Italy's Dallara Automobili, had invited Branam and Rose-Hulman engineering students to a Formula SAE event and a tour through state-of-the-art automotive technology at the Dallara facility. Read full interview with Branam and trustee Jeff Belskus of Indianapolis Speedway, Dallara Magazine p. 6.
